## Segment Structure Evolution

| Period | Segments |
|--------|----------|
| 2025 onward | CCG, DCAI, Intel Foundry (reportable); All Other (Mobileye + Other) |
| 2024 | CCG, DCAI, NEX, Intel Foundry (reportable); All Other (Altera + Mobileye + Other) |
| 2023 | CCG, DCAI, NEX, Mobileye, IFS (reportable: CCG, DCAI, NEX) |
| 2022 | CCG, DCAI, NEX, Mobileye, IFS; AXG integrated into CCG/DCAI in Q1 2023 |

**Key change Q1 2025:** NEX integrated into CCG and DCAI. Altera deconsolidated Sep 12, 2025 (51% sold). 2025 10-K retrospectively recast all prior periods to reflect new structure.

**Key change Q1 2024:** Internal foundry model introduced; Intel Products (CCG, DCAI, NEX) separated from Intel Foundry. Altera moved from DCAI to All Other.

### FY2026 CapEx Guidance (from Q1 2026 Earnings Call, April 23, 2026)
*Source: Intel Q1 2026 earnings transcript — CFO David Zinsner prepared remarks and Q&A*

| Metric | Guidance | Source Quote |
|---|---|---|
| **FY2026 Total CapEx** | **Flat YoY** vs FY2025 (~$17.7B gross) | "We forecast capital expenditures in 2026 to be flat to last year versus our prior expectation of flat to down, reflecting increased capacity investments to support committed demand" (Zinsner, prepared remarks) |
| **Tool spending** | **Up ~25% YoY** | "Tool spending will be up year over year 25% or so" (Zinsner, Q&A - Ben Reitzes) |
| **Space spending** | **Down materially** | "We actually will be bringing the space spend down pretty materially, even though the total is flat" (Zinsner, Q&A) |
| **Quarterly cadence** | **Roughly equal across year** | "We now expect expenditures to be roughly equal across the year and still to be heavily weighted towards the equipment that directly grows wafer outs" (Zinsner, prepared remarks) |
| **Q1 2026 Gross CapEx** | **$5.0B** (actual) | "Gross CapEx of $5 billion in the quarter" (Zinsner, prepared remarks). Confirmed via 10-Q: $3,636M cash CapEx + $1,327M financed = $4,963M ≈ $5.0B |
| **FY2026 Adjusted FCF** | **Positive** (ex-Fab 34 buyout) | "Excluding the buyout of the Fab 34 joint investment, we still expect positive adjusted free cash flow for the full year" (Zinsner, prepared remarks) |
| **CapEx drivers** | Capacity to support demand; equipment-heavy | "Reflecting increased capacity investments to support committed demand and a continued emphasis on improving fab productivity and output" |
| **FY2027+ CapEx** | TBD, tied to external customer signals | "As we get into next year, we'll have a better sense...customer signals would be more concrete in the back half of this year and into early next year" (Zinsner, Q&A) |

### Key Context for CapEx:
- **Shift from space to tools**: Prior years had heavy "space" (building/shell) spending. Now pivoting to equipment that directly increases wafer output. This means each CapEx dollar is more productive for near-term revenue.
- **Supply still below demand**: Zinsner noted undershipping demand by "a B" (billions), driving urgency for tool additions.
- **Fab 34 buyout**: ~$14.2B total ($7.7B cash + $6.5B new debt) in April 2026 to repurchase Apollo's 49% stake. This is a one-time financing/investing cash outflow, NOT CapEx.
